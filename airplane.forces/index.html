The Four Forces of Flight

Aerodynamics Guide

What are we modeling?

A paper airplane is a pure glider once thrown. This simulation models 2D aerodynamics with lift, drag, and gravity forces acting on the aircraft.

Phugoid Oscillations

Watch how your airplane climbs, slows, dips, and rises again—the classic phugoid mode:

  1. Thrown fast → high lift → plane climbs
  2. Climbs → slows down → kinetic → potential energy
  3. Slows → lift drops → nose dips under gravity
  4. Dive accelerates → rebuilds speed → cycle repeats

Key Parameters

  • Wing Area: More lift, but also more drag
  • Drag Coefficient: Low = dart (fast), High = glider (slow)
  • Wind: Headwind increases relative velocity and lift
